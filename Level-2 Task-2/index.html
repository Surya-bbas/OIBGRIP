Adolf Hitler

Murderer of Millions

Hitler saluting his army

  Hitler affected the world as a whole and was a main cause of WWII. He murdered millions of Jews and countless soldiers in his plan to control Europe. He almost did it, too. Below is a list of major events in Hitler's personal life.

  • April 20, 1889: Hitler is born to Alois and Klara Schicklgruber -- child #5.

    • Background: "... [Alois and Klara] had a few children together. They had a boy named Gustav, one named Otto, and a girl named Ida. All three died within a short time of their births" (Eric Graham). They then had a son (Alois Jr.) before having Adolf.

  • 1892: The Hitler family moves from Braunau Am Inn, Austria to an area near Linz, Austria.
  • 1894: Edmund is born-- Adolf's second brother.
  • 1895: Alois retires, forcing his children to cope with his drunkenness and constant anger.
  • 1895: Alois Jr. runs away from home
  • 1896:
    • The family moves a short distance to Lambach.
    • A daughter is born to the Hitler family: Paula
  • 1897: The family again moves, this time to Leonding.
  • 1900: Edmund dies.

    • Adolf becomes "detatched and introverted" and at this time he "... showed an early interest in German nationalism, rejecting the authority of Austria-Hungary. This nationalism would become the motivating force of Hitler's life" (historyplace.com)

  • 1903: Alois dies.
  • 1905: Adolf develops lung problems and drops out of school.
  • Adolf gets a few jobs and eventually loses all of them, leaving him homeless for eight years.
  • 1908: Adolf's mother dies
  • 1914: Hitler joins the army as is outraged at germany's surrender. he joins the German Worker's Party and climbs up the ranks until he becomes the chairman. Hitler became very popular with the people because of his great talent as an orator. The hardships citizens needed to cope with increased their openness to the idea Hitler proposed: a new government.
  • 1921: Hitler creates the Stormtruppen (storm troopers).
  • 1923: Hitler leads a failed coup with his SA (Stormtruppen). This means jail time for Hitler.
  • 1924: Hitler begins writing Mein Kamph, in which he describes his political ideas- one of which is the Holocaust. Another is his main plan for world domination: "The Germans would first fight in the south and move in to Czechoslovakia. They would then go back across Germany to fight France and Britain. When they won, the German army would again move across Germany to fight the Russians; and finally, they would defeat the United States" (Eric Graham).
  • 1928:
    • The Nazi party gets less than five percent of votes in a German election.
    • Hitler meets Eva Braun (his future wife).

    • At this time the Great Depression is affecting Germany and making life very difficult for the citizens, making far more convert to the Nazi party, looking for change.

  • 1932:
    • Hitler becomes the Chancellor of Germany.
    • German President Hindenburg dies; Hitler becomes president.

    • Hitler starts controlling the country. All political parties are eliminated except the Nazi party. The Enabling Act is passed along with other similar laws that give the government additional powers. Germany leaves the League of Nations and the army is built up. Finally, the Holocaust begins.

  • 1934: Hitler passes a law giving him the powers of the head of state (allowing him to control the army)
  • 1938: Germany invades Poland, starting the war officially.

    • Hitler, being stubborn as usual, refuses to back down when he begins to lose and punishes his generals that do.

  • 1944: The Allies enter France. Germany had the chance to stop them or slow them down, but Hitler was asleep when they got the news. Everyone was afraid of waking him, so nobody did.
  • 1945:
    • The Allies had taken France and started on Germany.
    • Hitler marries Eva Braun in a bunker.
    • The couple commits double suicide.
